Rocky Mountain Elk

Male elk have antlers they shed annually, making them non-acerous.

  • Scientific Name: Cervus canadensis nelsoni
  • Average weight (male): 320 to 331 kg
  • Average weight (female): 225 to 241 kg
  • Habitat: Rocky Mountains

Rocky Mountain elk are ruminates and feed on grasses, plants, leaves and bark. One of the largest members of the deer family, they run with a majestic grace.
